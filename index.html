Hello there,

I'm Ric, a 25 years old guy from Forlì, Italy, also known as

Riccardo Ghini!

I have a background in Mathematics and am currently diving into computer science and web devolpment. I have a deep passion for astrophysics, though my studies have taken me in a different direction for now. I also enjoy exploring history, particularly the medieval period.

I’m an avid reader who devours fantasy, sci-fi, and the occasional historical book or novel of course. I have a deep passion for roleplaying games -Dungeons & Dragons is my go to lately- and I spend plenty of time drawing worlds -both on paper and in my mind.

I love board games too, though these days I rarely get to play them as often as I used to. You know, with a larger circle of friends and fewer free nights, those classic 4-player games are a rare treat. I also enjoy video games, LEGO, and planning trips, too many actually for my finances.

When I’m not immersed in creative or gaming endeavors, you can usually find me at the gym or honing my skills in medieval sword fencing —discipline that perfectly complements my love for all things medieval. My weekends are a whirlwind of activity as I volunteer as a scout leader in the parish near my home.

I'm always on the lookout for interesting projects, whether it's coding, writing, or just diving into a new subject. I listen to music while working, but I wouldn't call myself an expert on any particular genre.

Bachelor's Degree Thesis

📄View Full Thesis

This thesis explores Gravitational Lensing as a tool for detecting Dark Matter, which makes up most of the Universe’s matter but cannot be directly observed. After introducing key concepts, the three main types of lensing (strong, weak, and micro) are examined with practical applications, including dark matter evidence in the Bullet Cluster. Lastly, the thesis discusses the necessity of dark matter, potential candidates, and alternative theories proposed over time.
